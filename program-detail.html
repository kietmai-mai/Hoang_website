Conventional Loans

Flexible financing options with competitive rates and terms for qualified borrowers

Program Overview

Conventional loans are mortgage loans that are not insured or guaranteed by the federal government. They offer flexibility and competitive rates for borrowers with good credit and stable income.

3%

Who This Program Is For

Ideal candidates for conventional loan programs

Good Credit History

Borrowers with credit scores of 620 or higher who have established credit history and manageable debt levels.

Stable Income

Applicants with steady employment history and sufficient income to comfortably afford monthly mortgage payments.

Down Payment Ready

Buyers who can provide a down payment of at least 3% of the purchase price, with options to avoid PMI with 20% down.

Key Benefits

Why choose a conventional loan for your home purchase

Flexible Loan Terms

Choose from 10, 15, 20, or 30-year fixed-rate terms, or opt for adjustable-rate mortgages (ARMs) for initial savings.

No Mortgage Insurance with 20% Down

Avoid monthly PMI payments by putting down 20% or more, potentially saving hundreds per month compared to other loan types.

Higher Loan Limits

Conventional loans offer higher borrowing limits than FHA loans, making them ideal for more expensive properties.

Competitive Interest Rates

With good credit, conventional loans often offer the most competitive interest rates available in the market.

Flexible Property Types

Finance primary residences, second homes, and investment properties with conventional loan programs.

Faster Processing

Without government involvement, conventional loans often process faster than FHA or VA loans.
